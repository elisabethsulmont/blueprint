Blueprint

navigating interior spaces

Why wayfinding?

Although navigation is one of the oldest problems humans throughout history have faced, it remains a core obstacle in terms of usability for large, man-made structures. Hospitals, museums, airports, and universities all present unique opportunities for improved spatial understanding because of their complex organization and accessibility needs. Building layouts are rarely available online, and many physically disabled individuals are left guessing whether or not they will be able to reach a certain destination. Simply locating a restroom is sometimes a near-impossible task.

Brown Student Services Building

Motivation for the project

Based on the difficulties we personally experienced wayfinding on McGill campus, we selected the Brown Building as the focus for our project. This building is the home of nine distinct student services, including Student Health Services, International Student Services, Counselling, and Career Planning, just to name a few. In the spirit of this year's theme, "enhancing us as humans", we will be attempting to improve human-spatial understanding and navigation using the principles of human-computer interaction. Not only does this topic suit the course requirements well, it may help students find appropriate aid in less time and with less confusion.
